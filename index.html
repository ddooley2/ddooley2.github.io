Research

My research aims to develop next-generation CRISPR-Cas antimicrobials for combatting virulent bacterial pathogens. Specifically, I employ computational and experimental techniques to:

  1. Develop novel gRNA design solutions for selectively removing a bacterial pathogen from a commensal microbiome.
  2. Construct and characterize orthogonal antimicrobial formulations with improved efficacy and mitigated resistance.
  3. Improve phage-based packing and delivery of CRISPR payloads.
  4. Understand genotype-phenotype connections using integrated OMICS analyses.

Broadly, I am curious about the systems-level interactions that govern relationships between bacterial hosts and natural/engineered phages. Some questions I currently find interesting are:

  • How does genetic organization and regulation of (pro)phage genomes impact lysis/lysogeny decisions?
  • How do interactions between (pro)phage and host proteins/RNAs influence host metabolism, phage treatment efficacy, and rise of resistance?
  • How are host proteomes/transcriptomes/metabolomes dynamically reallocated in response to natural/engineered phage attack?
